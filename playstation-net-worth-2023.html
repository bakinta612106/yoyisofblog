Beginning Of Playstation

PlayStation is the creation of Ken Kutaragi, a former Sony executive. While working as an engineer at Sony, he had the idea, which he then put into action, resulting in the creation of PlayStation. Sony once had a relationship with Nintendo, but it ended when they couldn’t agree on how to split revenue. Kutaragi was tasked with creating the PlayStation project to compete with Nintendo.

Some members of Sony’s board of directors were opposed to the brand’s development. As a result, Kutaragi and his staff were forced to relocate the company’s headquarters to Sony Music. The brand’s logo was created by Manabu Sakamoto. The first PlayStation was released in Japan in December 1994, and it was quickly followed by numerous upgrades such as the ‘PS One,’ Net Yaroze, and the PocketStation. The PlayStation One and PlayStation 2 consoles were the first to sell more than 120 million units.

Evolution Of PlayStation

The PlayStation One was released on July 7, 2000, as a smaller version of the original PlayStation. The console was a huge success, outselling all of its successors. The PlayStation 2 debuted in 2000 and is backward compatible with a variety of PlayStation games. With the most units sold, this was the most popular console in the world.

The PlayStation 2 was redesigned as a slimline model in 2004. This model was thinner, smaller, quieter, and included a built-in port than its predecessors. It was updated in 2007 to be even lighter, with a smaller AC adaptor. The model was updated again in 2008, this time with less weight. In Japan, the PlayStation 3 was released in November 2006.

One of the new model’s standout features was the use of motion-sensing technology. In 2009, it was revised, and a slim version was made available. The new model was smaller, lighter, and used significantly less energy than previous models. After three years on the market, the model was redesigned to be much slimmer.

On February 20, 2013, the PlayStation 4 was released. This was the eighth-generation system from the company, and it was the first to use x86 architecture. With the PlayStation App, gamers could use tablets and cellphones as second screens with the system. The small version of the console was released on September 7, 2016. The pro model, which debuted on September 7, 2017, was redesigned further. The PlayStation 5, the most recent PlayStation console, will be released in 2020.

Awards

Since its inception, PlayStation has seen tremendous growth and development. It was no surprise, then, that they won the Technology & Engineering Emmy Award for Outstanding Achievement in Video Gaming Technology and Applications in both 2005 and 2006. According to Guinness World Records, PlayStation became the best-selling video game home console brand in history in 2019. In 1999, the brand received the British Academy Games Award.
