Sara Malakul Lane is a well-known Thai-American actress and former supermodel. Moreover, Sara Malakul Lane is best known for King Arthur’s Sword (2017), Jailbird (2014), Kickboxer: Vengeance (2016), and Who’s Watching Oliver (2017).

Early Life

Sara Malakul Lane was born On the 1st of February 1983, in Guam. She is 40 years old as of 2023 and holds Thai-American nationality. Meanwhile, she has a birth sign of Aquarius and belongs to a mixed ethnicity.

She was born to a Scottish father, Alastair Lane, and a Thai mother, Thapthim Malakul na Ayudhaya. On her mother’s side, she belongs to the Chakri Dynasty, the present reigning dynasty in Thailand.

Her mother’s father Mom Luang Pikthip Malakul was a diplomat; an ambassador of Thailand to Myanmar and the United Kingdom. As a child, Sara grew up traveling between the United Kingdom and Bangkok. Currently, she resides in Singapore.

Professional Career

Sara Malakul Lane began her modeling career at the age of 14 and later moved to Thailand to pursue her acting career at the age of 15. Back in 2000, she made her television debut in the TV mini-series “Hua Jai Song Park” playing the role of Sunny (Tawanshine). Initially, she gained popularity in Thailand for her roles in movies such as “Pee Mak” and “Sunset at Chaophraya.”

She did her first role in a Hollywood film playing Steven Seagal’s daughter in the 2003 film, “Belly of the Beast.” After two, she appeared as Angela in the Bulgarian direct-to-video film “The Volcano Disaster.”

In 2010, she was cast as Nicole Sands in the SyFy horror/science fiction film “Sharktopus” opposite Eric Roberts. She played the role of Veronica in the 2012 American horror film “12/12/12.” In 2014, she played the role of Samorn in the Thai-American supernatural horror film “Pernicious.”

From 2015 to 2018, she appeared in several films such as “Jurassic City”(2015), “Scouts Guide to the Zombie Apocalypse” (2015), “Buddy Hutchins” (2015), “Beyond the Gates” (2016), “Who’s Watching Oliver” (2017), and “Halloween Pussy Trap Kill! Kill!” (2017).

Back in 2016, she played a Bangkok detective Liu who became the love interest of the lead character in “Kickboxer: Vengeance.” The movie was released in September 2016. Later, she again reprised the role of Liu in its sequel movie “Kickboxer: Retaliation” which was released in 2018.

She has also appeared in short films such as “Baby, We’ll Be Fine” (2011), and “Disposition” (2018).

Award

Till now, this actress has won one award and one nomination. In 2017, she won Best Supporting Actress for her work in the movie “Who’s Watching Oliver” at the Vancouver Badass Film Festival. That same year, she was nominated for the Best Supporting role in the same movie at the Fright Night Theatre Film Festival (FNTFF).
