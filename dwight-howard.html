Career

In 2004, the Orlando Magic selected Dwight Howard with the first overall pick in the NBA draft. He would have become a free agent at the conclusion of his final season with the Orlando Magic had he not been traded to the Brooklyn Nets. On August 10, 2012, he was traded to the Los Angeles Lakers. The Los Angeles Lakers also acquired Chris Duhon and Earl Clark at the time.

In early July, Howard appeared in games for the Los Angeles Lakers, Dallas Mavericks, Houston Rockets, Golden State Warriors, and Atlanta Hawks. He did, however, intend to join the Rockets and signed with the team on July 13, 2013.

On July 12, 2016, Howard agreed to a three-year, $70 million contract with the Atlanta Hawks. He entered the 2016–2017 season as the NBA’s active leader in rebounds and blocked shots following Tim Duncan’s retirement, and made his debut in the inaugural season.

Howard was acquired by the Charlotte Hornets on June 20, 2017, in exchange for Marco Belinelli and Miles Plumlee. He was acquired by the Brooklyn Nets in exchange for Timofey Mozgov, but the Nets immediately waived him. On July 12, 2018, the Washington Wizards signed Dwight Howard as a power forward, and he is now a member of the team. He is dressed in a number 21 shirt.
